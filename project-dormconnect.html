01

Project overview

Understanding the challenge

The problem

Students looking for accommodation around KMITL often rely on fragmented information from social media, recommendations, and separate dormitory pages.

Information can be incomplete or difficult to compare, especially when students need to consider price, facilities, location, travel distance, and reviews at the same time.

Design challenge

How might we help students confidently find and compare suitable dormitories without switching between multiple information sources?
01

Scattered information

Students must search through several channels before finding enough information to make a decision.

02

Difficult comparison

Prices, facilities, room types, and locations are presented inconsistently.

03

Low decision confidence

Limited visual information and unclear details make it difficult for students to shortlist options.

02

Discover

Learning from target users

Research objective

I conducted a user survey to understand how students currently search for dormitories, which information affects their decisions, and which parts of the existing search process cause confusion.

Method Online user survey
Participants [Add actual number]
Target users KMITL students

Research questions

  • Where do students currently search for dormitory information?
  • Which factors are most important when choosing a dormitory?
  • What information is often missing or difficult to verify?
  • What makes comparing multiple dormitories difficult?
Insight 01

Students need one reliable source

Switching between multiple sources increases search time and makes information harder to verify.

Insight 02

Location must be easy to understand

Students need clear distance and location information to assess daily travel convenience.

Insight 03

Comparison supports decision-making

Users need to compare essential information without repeatedly opening different pages.

Insight 04

Filters should reflect real priorities

Price, facilities, room type, and location are important factors when narrowing down options.

KMITL student looking for accommodation

Find a suitable dormitory that matches budget and lifestyle needs.

Frustration

Incomplete information and inconsistent presentation across platforms.

Need

A quick and trustworthy way to shortlist and compare options.

Problem statement

KMITL students need a centralized way to discover and compare nearby dormitories because fragmented information makes the current decision-making process slow, confusing, and unreliable.

Ideate

Structuring the experience

The solution was organized around the core tasks students need to complete: discover relevant dormitories, understand key information, save options, and compare shortlisted properties.

⌕

Search and filters

Narrow results by price, location, facilities, and room preferences.

⌖

Location information

Help students understand proximity and travel convenience.

♡

Save options

Let students build a shortlist without repeating the search process.

⇄

Compare dormitories

Present essential information side by side to support confident decisions.

Primary user flow

Open platform → Search or filter → View details → Save options → Compare → Make a decision

Reflection

What I learned

This project strengthened my ability to connect user research with interface decisions and front-end implementation. It also showed me the importance of organizing complex information around the user's primary decision-making needs.

What I would improve next

  • Conduct additional usability testing with a larger participant group.
  • Validate the usefulness and credibility of user reviews.
  • Improve map-based exploration and travel-time information.
  • Add accessibility testing with keyboard and screen-reader users.
